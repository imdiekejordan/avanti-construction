What is the Most Expensive Part of a Bathroom Remodel?

December 29, 2025 • 8 min read

Planning a bathroom remodel involves many decisions, and understanding where your money goes is crucial for effective budgeting. While every project is different, certain elements consistently account for the largest portion of bathroom renovation costs. Knowing these expenses upfront can help you make informed decisions and prioritize where to invest your budget.

The Cost Breakdown: What Takes the Biggest Bite?

On average, a mid-range bathroom remodel costs between $10,000 and $25,000, while high-end remodels can reach $50,000 or more. Here's how those costs typically break down:

1. Labor Costs: 40-60% of Total Budget

Labor is almost always the most expensive component of a bathroom remodel, typically accounting for 40-60% of your total budget. This includes:

  • General contractor fees
  • Plumber costs ($45-$200 per hour)
  • Electrician costs ($50-$150 per hour)
  • Tile installer fees ($4-$14 per square foot)
  • Drywall and painting labor
  • Carpentry work

Why labor costs so much: Bathroom renovations require multiple skilled tradespeople, and plumbing and electrical work must meet strict building codes. Additionally, the complexity of working in small spaces and coordinating multiple trades drives up costs.

2. Plumbing and Fixtures: 15-20% of Total Budget

The second-largest expense is typically plumbing work and fixtures. This category includes:

  • Shower systems: $500-$8,000+ (standard to luxury)
  • Bathtubs: $400-$6,000+
  • Toilets: $150-$2,000+
  • Vanities and sinks: $300-$5,000+
  • Faucets and fixtures: $150-$2,500+ each
  • Plumbing rough-in labor: $1,000-$5,000+

Moving existing plumbing (like relocating a toilet or shower) significantly increases costs, as this requires opening walls, rerouting pipes, and potentially upgrading your plumbing system.

3. Cabinetry and Vanity: 10-15% of Total Budget

Bathroom vanities and storage solutions can be a significant investment:

  • Stock vanities: $300-$1,500
  • Semi-custom vanities: $1,000-$4,000
  • Custom vanities: $2,500-$10,000+
  • Medicine cabinets: $200-$2,000+
  • Storage solutions: $500-$3,000+

4. Countertops: 5-10% of Total Budget

Bathroom countertops vary widely in price:

  • Laminate: $20-$50 per square foot
  • Solid surface: $40-$100 per square foot
  • Quartz: $50-$150 per square foot
  • Granite: $40-$200 per square foot
  • Marble: $75-$250+ per square foot

5. Flooring and Tile: 5-10% of Total Budget

Tile is a popular choice for bathrooms and costs include both materials and installation:

  • Ceramic tile: $1-$15 per square foot (plus $4-$14/sq ft installation)
  • Porcelain tile: $3-$20 per square foot (plus installation)
  • Natural stone: $5-$35+ per square foot (plus installation)
  • Luxury vinyl: $2-$7 per square foot (plus installation)

Keep in mind that tile installation in bathrooms requires waterproofing, which adds to labor costs.

What Makes a Bathroom Remodel More Expensive?

Moving Plumbing Fixtures

One of the biggest cost drivers is moving existing plumbing. If you want to relocate your toilet, sink, or shower, expect to pay significantly more:

  • Moving a toilet: $500-$2,000+
  • Moving a shower: $1,500-$5,000+
  • Moving a sink: $300-$1,500+

This requires opening walls, rerouting pipes, and may involve upgrading your plumbing system to meet current codes.

Electrical Upgrades

Modern bathrooms require proper electrical work, which can add substantial costs:

  • GFCI outlets (required): $150-$250 each
  • Vent fan installation/upgrade: $300-$1,000+
  • Lighting upgrades: $500-$3,000+
  • Heated floors: $5-$15 per square foot
  • Electrical panel upgrades (if needed): $1,500-$4,000+

Structural Changes

Any changes to walls, floors, or the structure itself will increase costs:

  • Removing walls: $300-$2,000+
  • Expanding the bathroom footprint: $5,000-$15,000+
  • Reinforcing floors (for heavy tubs): $1,000-$5,000+

High-End Materials and Fixtures

Luxury finishes can dramatically increase your budget:

  • Premium faucets and fixtures: Can exceed $1,000 each
  • High-end shower systems: $2,000-$15,000+
  • Designer tiles: $20-$100+ per square foot
  • Custom millwork: $5,000-$20,000+
"Labor typically accounts for 40-60% of a bathroom remodel budget, making it the single largest expense. Quality labor is worth the investment, as proper installation prevents costly repairs down the road."

How to Save Money on Your Bathroom Remodel

While you can't eliminate major costs, you can make smart choices to keep your budget in check:

Keep Plumbing in Place

The easiest way to save money is to keep your existing plumbing layout. Instead of moving fixtures, consider:

  • Updating fixtures in their current locations
  • Refinishing or resurfacing existing tubs/showers
  • Replacing rather than relocating

Choose Mid-Range Materials

You don't need to go budget, but avoiding ultra-premium materials can save thousands:

  • Choose quartz over marble
  • Select quality but not luxury fixtures
  • Consider porcelain tile that mimics natural stone
  • Opt for semi-custom over fully custom cabinetry

Prioritize Your Spending

Invest in areas that matter most to you:

  • If you love long showers, invest in a quality shower system
  • If storage is important, prioritize custom cabinetry
  • If aesthetics matter most, focus on tile and finishes

Do Some Work Yourself (Carefully)

Some tasks can be DIY-friendly if you're handy:

  • Demolition (if no hazardous materials)
  • Painting
  • Installing accessories (towel bars, etc.)
  • Simple tiling (with proper research)

Important: Never attempt plumbing or electrical work unless you're licensed. Mistakes can be dangerous and costly to fix.

Typical Bathroom Remodel Costs by Scope

Budget Remodel ($3,000-$7,000)

  • Cosmetic updates only
  • Keep existing layout
  • Refinish rather than replace
  • Stock fixtures and materials

Mid-Range Remodel ($10,000-$25,000)

  • Replace fixtures and surfaces
  • Update plumbing and electrical
  • New vanity and countertops
  • Quality tile and finishes

High-End Remodel ($25,000-$50,000+)

  • Complete gut and redesign
  • Custom everything
  • Luxury fixtures and materials
  • Structural changes
  • Smart features and technology

Conclusion

While labor costs typically represent the largest expense in a bathroom remodel (40-60% of your budget), plumbing and fixtures are usually the second-highest cost. Understanding where your money goes helps you make informed decisions and set realistic expectations for your renovation.

The key to a successful bathroom remodel is balancing your desires with your budget.
