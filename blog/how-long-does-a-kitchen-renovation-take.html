How Long Does a Kitchen Renovation Take?

December 29, 2025 • 10 min read

One of the most common questions homeowners ask when planning a kitchen renovation is, "How long will this take?" The answer depends on several factors, including the scope of work, complexity of the design, and whether you're working with professionals or going the DIY route. Understanding the timeline will help you plan accordingly and set realistic expectations for your project.

Typical Kitchen Renovation Timelines

The duration of a kitchen renovation can vary significantly based on the project's complexity. Here's what you can generally expect:

Minor Kitchen Updates (2-4 weeks)

If you're making cosmetic changes without moving plumbing or electrical, your renovation will be relatively quick. These projects typically include:

  • Cabinet refacing or painting
  • Countertop replacement
  • Backsplash installation
  • Fixture and hardware updates
  • Flooring replacement (if no structural work needed)

Mid-Range Kitchen Renovations (6-12 weeks)

Most standard kitchen renovations fall into this category. These projects involve more substantial work and typically take:

  • Planning and design: 2-4 weeks
  • Demolition: 1-3 days
  • Structural work (if needed): 1-2 weeks
  • Plumbing and electrical rough-in: 1-2 weeks
  • Drywall and insulation: 1 week
  • Cabinet installation: 1-2 weeks
  • Countertop installation: 1-2 weeks (includes measurement and fabrication)
  • Flooring installation: 1 week
  • Backsplash and finishing touches: 1 week
  • Final inspections: 3-5 days

Major Kitchen Renovations (12-20 weeks or more)

Extensive renovations that involve structural changes, layout modifications, or custom features will take longer:

  • Complete kitchen gut and rebuild
  • Moving walls or structural modifications
  • Custom cabinetry and millwork
  • High-end appliances requiring special installation
  • Complex electrical and plumbing upgrades
  • Multiple permit approvals and inspections

Factors That Affect Your Kitchen Renovation Timeline

1. Permits and Approvals

Depending on your local jurisdiction, obtaining permits can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks. In San Diego County, permit processing times vary by city:

  • Simple permit applications: 5-10 business days
  • Complex projects requiring plan review: 2-6 weeks
  • Projects requiring structural engineering: Additional 1-2 weeks

2. Custom Orders and Material Availability

Custom cabinets, specialty appliances, and unique materials can significantly extend your timeline:

  • Stock cabinets: Available immediately or within 1-2 weeks
  • Semi-custom cabinets: 4-8 weeks
  • Custom cabinets: 8-16 weeks
  • High-end appliances: 8-16 weeks (some manufacturers are currently experiencing longer lead times)
  • Natural stone countertops: 2-4 weeks for fabrication after template
  • Quartz countertops: 1-3 weeks for fabrication

3. Scope of Work

The more extensive your renovation, the longer it will take. Consider these factors:

  • Are you changing the layout or keeping everything in place?
  • Do you need to move plumbing or electrical?
  • Are you removing walls or making structural changes?
  • Will you be upgrading your HVAC system?
  • Are you adding windows or skylights?

4. Contractor Availability and Scheduling

Your timeline also depends on:

  • Contractor's current workload and availability
  • Seasonal factors (construction season peaks can cause delays)
  • Subcontractor coordination (plumbers, electricians, etc.)
  • Weather conditions (for projects involving exterior work)

5. Unforeseen Issues

It's wise to budget extra time for unexpected problems that may arise:

  • Hidden water damage or mold
  • Outdated or non-compliant electrical wiring
  • Plumbing issues discovered during demolition
  • Structural problems that need addressing
  • Asbestos or lead paint (common in older homes)

The Kitchen Renovation Process: Week by Week

Weeks 1-2: Planning and Design

Before any work begins, you'll spend time:

  • Meeting with your contractor and designer
  • Finalizing your design and layout
  • Selecting materials, fixtures, and appliances
  • Obtaining necessary permits
  • Ordering materials with long lead times

Weeks 3-4: Preparation and Demolition

Once permits are approved, work begins:

  • Protecting adjacent areas of your home
  • Disconnecting utilities
  • Removing old cabinets, appliances, and fixtures
  • Demolishing walls if layout changes are planned
  • Preparing the space for new construction

Weeks 5-7: Structural, Plumbing, and Electrical

The "guts" of your kitchen are installed:

  • Framing and structural work (if needed)
  • Rough plumbing installation
  • Rough electrical work
  • HVAC adjustments (if needed)
  • Initial inspections

Weeks 8-10: Drywall, Insulation, and Paint

Your kitchen starts taking shape:

  • Insulation installation
  • Drywall hanging and finishing
  • Priming and painting
  • Ceiling work

Weeks 11-14: Finishes Installation

The exciting part where you see your vision come to life:

  • Cabinet installation
  • Countertop templating and installation
  • Backsplash installation
  • Flooring installation

Weeks 15-16: Final Details

Completing the finishing touches:

  • Appliance installation
  • Plumbing fixture installation
  • Lighting installation
  • Hardware installation
  • Final inspections
  • Touch-ups and cleanup

How to Speed Up Your Kitchen Renovation

While you can't control everything, there are steps you can take to help your project stay on track:

  • Choose stock or quick-ship items: Avoid custom orders when possible
  • Make decisions quickly: Delays in material selection can push back your timeline
  • Hire experienced professionals: An experienced contractor can anticipate issues and solve problems efficiently
  • Plan for living arrangements: If you'll be displaced, arrange temporary kitchen facilities
  • Communicate regularly: Stay in touch with your contractor to address issues quickly
  • Be flexible: Minor adjustments may be needed along the way

What About DIY Kitchen Renovations?

If you're doing the work yourself, your timeline can be significantly longer. DIY renovations often take:

  • Weekends-only work: 3-6 months or more
  • Evenings and weekends: 2-4 months
  • Full-time DIY: 4-8 weeks (but you'll need to account for the learning curve)

Remember: DIY projects often take longer than expected, especially if you encounter problems or need to learn new skills. Professional contractors have the experience, tools, and resources to work more efficiently.

Conclusion

While every kitchen renovation is unique, most projects take between 6 and 12 weeks from start to finish. By working with experienced professionals, planning ahead, and accounting for material lead times, you can help ensure your project stays on schedule.

The most important factor in maintaining your timeline is choosing the right contractor. Look for a licensed, insured professional with a proven track record of completing projects on time and within budget.
